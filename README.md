# Online Fashion Purchasing Behavior Analysis

## Business Problem
Online fashion platforms face increasing competition, making it essential to understand the key factors influencing consumers’ purchasing decisions. This project analyzes consumer behavior to identify important marketing factors that influence online fashion purchasing decisions and provide insights to support better business strategies.

## Objectives
* To analyze online fashion purchasing behavior.
* To identify key marketing factors (Product, Price, Place, Promotion) influencing purchase decisions.
* To segment consumers based on their online fashion purchasing behavior.

## Business Questions
* Which marketing factors most influence online fashion purchasing decisions?
* Are there distinct groups of consumers with different purchasing behaviors?
* How can online platforms use consumer behavior insights to improve marketing strategies?

## Methodology
The analysis was conducted using the following techniques:
* **One-way ANOVA** to compare differences in purchasing behavior across different consumer groups
* **K-means Clustering** to segment consumers based on purchasing behavior
* **Random Forest** to identify the most important factors influencing purchase decisions
* **Ordinal Logistic Regression** to analyze relationships between marketing factors and purchase decisions

## Dataset
The dataset was collected through a survey on online fashion purchasing behavior.
It includes variables related to marketing factors such as **Product, Price, Place, and Promotion**, as well as consumer purchase decision levels.

## Tools
* Excel (data preparation)
* Python / Google Colab (Pandas, Scikit-learn)
* Statistical analysis

## Expected Insights
The analysis aims to identify key marketing factors influencing online fashion purchasing decisions and reveal distinct consumer segments with different purchasing behaviors. These insights can support more effective marketing strategies for online platforms.
